Fuse Jam #04 Themes



At Fuse Jam each team or individual has their own unique theme to spark ideas for coming up with a game. In Fuse Jam #04 we are going digital, with the digital aspect being central to the jam and also the theme itself!

For the first half of the theme we want to celebrate something many of us use every day - the Emoji! In some ways emojis can seen as a universal language of the Internet (even if the meanings may be different in different countries!), allowing us to use symbols to communicate globally. What do these symbols mean to you and how do you interpret them?

For the second half of the theme we want to celebrate somethings a bit different. The Internet is a vibrant place full of creativity and knowledge, but it can also be a very transitory place - fragile to changes in hosting, code updates and technology. Countless on- and offline digital works have already been lost to time. The Internet Archive is one place that aims to preserve our digital history, providing free access to user-uploaded and automatically-collected archived digital material. Thanks to websites like these we now have access to fantastic relics of computing... such as the source of the second half or our theme - freeware CD-ROMs that came free with computer magazines! The particular one we are using here is titled '5,000 animations', and does indeed contain 5000 animated GIFs.
